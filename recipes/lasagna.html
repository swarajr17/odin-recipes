Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish that has gained worldwide popularity for its rich, layered flavors. It is a baked pasta dish made up of alternating layers of flat, wide pasta sheets, sauces, cheeses, and fillings. The pasta sheets are traditionally made from durum wheat flour and eggs. The dish typically includes a tomato-based meat sauce or a vegetarian sauce, combined with cheeses like ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan. Lasagna is assembled in layers and baked in the oven until the top is golden and bubbly. It originated in Italy, specifically in the Emilia-Romagna region, and has become a beloved comfort food around the world.

Ingredients

  • Meat
  • Onion and garlic
  • Tomato products
  • Sugar
  • Spices and seasonings
  • Lasagna noodles
  • Cheeses
  • Egg

Steps

  1. Make the meat sauce.
  2. Cook the noodles.
  3. Make the ricotta mixture.
  4. Layer the lasagna according to the recipe instructions.
  5. Cover with foil and bake.
  6. Let the lasagna rest before serving.
